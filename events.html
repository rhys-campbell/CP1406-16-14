Planned events

Annual General Meeting

Saturday, 7th May at 2pm

North Queensland Opera and Music Theatre Hall in Gill Park

Our Annual General Meeting is open to the public. The meeting will be followed by refreshments and a Forum giving people the opportunity to put forward ideas about the orchestra, possible changes to up coming events and a chance to organize new events.

Twilight Classics

Sunday, July 17th at 5pm

Townsville Civic Theatre - 41 Boundary St, South Townsville

Conducted by Mario Dobering; the concert will feature the winner of the 2015 ACVC (Australian Concerto and Vocal Competition) Competition. The winner Arthur Athan from Sydney will play the Rachmaninov Piano Concerto No 2. It will also include Brahms Symphony No 4 and Queens Gardens written by Dr David Salisbury from James Cook Uni.

Queens Gardens Concert - The 1812 again!

Sunday, July 31st 3pm

Queens Gardens - Kennedy St, North Ward, Townsville

Free concert in celebration of the 150th Anniversary of the municipality of Townsville, co-presented with the Townsville City Council and hosted by Steve ‘Pricey’ Price. The program includes Vivaldi’s Spring from The Four Seasons performed by the effervescent British violinist Tasmin Little, appearances by local groups the Barrier Reef Orchestra and 1RAR band, and will finish with the audience ‘firing the cannons’ in Tchaikovsky’s 1812 Overture! Bring your picnic blanket and enjoy a truly delightful afternoon in the park.

Barrier Reef Orchestra Goes to the Opera

Saturday, October 22nd at 7.30pm

Townsville Civic Theatre - 41 Boundary St, South Townsville

This one is going to be a splash the Barrier Reef Orchestra is going to the Opera. The concert will have Craig Johnson as Conductor and will feature singer, Paul Tabone who grew up in Innisfail and now sings in Italy. This concert will be repeated in the Ingham Town Hall on Sunday, 29th at 3pm
