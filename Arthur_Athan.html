Arthur Athan Bio

 Twenty-three year old Arthur Athan is currently studying piano with Tamara Smolyar at Monash University, Melbourne, Australia. He has received many accolades, including first prize and Audience Choice Award at the 2015 Australian Concerto and Vocal Competition, the inaugural Spivakovsky Prize (2015) and a perfect score in his Music Performance studies (2011). Most recently, Arthur was selected for the Lev Vlassenko Piano Competition (2015).

Arthur made his concerto debut in 2014 performing Tchaikovsky’s Piano Concerto No 1 with the Old Scotch Symphony Orchestra under John Ferguson. He reprised this performance with the Monash Academy Orchestra under Fabian Russell in 2015 to a sold-out audience.

His former teachers include Majella Drew and Sergei Suetin. He has performed for artists including Paul Badura-Skoda, Leslie Howard, Natasha Vlassenko, Nicolas Hodges and Roberto Prosseda.

Arthur will appear with the Barrier Reef Orchestra in Townsville and the Metropolitan Symphony Orchestra in Perth playing Rachmaninoff in the 2016- 2017 season. He has also been selected for the 2016 Keri Keri International Piano Competition in New Zealand. Arthur continues his concurrent study in law and languages.
